## Subjective

**Chief Complaint:** Generalized fatigue/weakness; admission for skilled nursing care to rebuild strength prior to returning home.

**History of Present Illness:**
Patient is a 75-year-old woman transferred from the hospital for skilled nursing/rehabilitation. She reports feeling easily worn out, including after the ~20-minute transport ride. She describes:
- **Polydipsia:** Constant thirst, refilling drinks throughout the day; increased noticeably over the last few weeks (corroborated by daughter).
- **Polyuria:** Waking 4–5 times nightly to urinate.
- **Polyphagia with weakness:** Eating more than usual yet feeling "emptied out" and weak.
- **Peripheral tingling/paresthesias:** Bilateral feet and, to a lesser degree, hands; "pins and needles," worse at night; crept up gradually over months.
- **Daytime somnolence:** Napping much of the day, which is out of character per daughter.
- **Cognitive changes:** Reports feeling "foggy" and occasional word-finding difficulty that resolves.

Patient acknowledges difficulty maintaining a proper diabetic diet at home and admits to "letting things slide." She lives alone; daughter lives ~40 minutes away.

**Past Medical History:**

- Chronic kidney disease, stage 1 (diabetic; early)
- Hypertension
- Anemia (receiving scheduled vitamin B12 injections)

**Medications:**

1. **Type 2 diabetes mellitus, likely poorly controlled** — Symptomatic hyperglycemia suggested by polydipsia, polyuria, polyphagia, fatigue, and progressive peripheral paresthesias (possible diabetic peripheral neuropathy). Historically suboptimal home dietary/medication adherence.
2. **Diabetic chronic kidney disease, stage 1.**
3. **Hypertension** — on metoprolol.
4. **Anemia** — on scheduled vitamin B12 injections; dose due soon.
5. **Hypertriglyceridemia / metabolic syndrome.**
6. **Deconditioning/fatigue** — requiring rehabilitation to restore independent function.
7. **Mild cognitive/word-finding complaints** — possibly related to recent hospitalization and hyperglycemia.
8. **Multiple allergies** (aspirin, peanuts, animal dander) requiring facility precautions.

- Keep Thursday therapy dog off patient's hallway (animal dander).

**Care coordination**
