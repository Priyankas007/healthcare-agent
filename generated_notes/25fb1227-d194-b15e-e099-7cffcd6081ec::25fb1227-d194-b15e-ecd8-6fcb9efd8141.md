# Hospice Initial Assessment Note

## Subjective

**Chief Concern:** Hospice admission for comfort-focused care in the setting of advanced colon cancer.

**History of Present Illness:**
Ms. Nola is a 75-year-old woman with metastatic/advanced colon cancer presenting for hospice initial assessment in her home. She reports a long history of colonic polyps requiring repeated removal, progressing over the past year to invasive cancer now involving a "whole stretch of the colon." Her specialist advised last month that further disease-directed treatment would cause more harm than benefit; the patient elected to forgo further curative/aggressive treatment and pursue comfort-focused care. She states she is "done fighting" but "not done living."

**Goals of Care (in patient's words):** Remain at home; enjoy her flower beds, her television programs, her niece Karen, and her cat (Biscuit); avoid dying in a hospital; and minimize pain. Patient clearly declines hospitalization and aggressive interventions.

**Symptoms:**
- **Pain:** Dull, low abdominal pain, intermittent, most days. Chronic low back pain for ~20 years (baseline). Some days both worsen together. Currently controlled to "just about bearable" on medications. At time of visit, pain reported as "about as good as it gets."
- **Bowels:** Slower than baseline but moving most days; encouraged prune juice/fluids by niece. No report of obstipation currently.
- **Appetite:** Poor; "eats like a bird."
- **Energy:** Fatigued; sleeping most afternoons. History of anemia, which patient feels may contribute.

**Code Status / Advance Care Preferences:** Patient clearly states she wants no resuscitation, no machines, no chest compressions ("let me go easy"). Wishes have been longstanding and are known to her niece, who will honor them.

**Social/Support:** Niece Karen has been staying overnight most nights and manages medications/paperwork. Patient is supported and comfortable at home. Family expresses relief and support of the hospice plan.

## Assessment

75-year-old woman with advanced colon cancer, no longer pursuing disease-directed therapy, appropriate for and electing home hospice care. Two-physician hospice certification completed with attending physician. Patient has clear, consistent goals focused on comfort at home and avoidance of hospitalization/resuscitation.

Active issues:
1. **Advanced colon cancer** — comfort-focused management.
2. **Pain** (abdominal cancer-related and chronic low back) — currently controlled but on a complex, redundant analgesic regimen requiring reconciliation.
3. **Opioid-related constipation risk** — ongoing monitoring required.
4. **Poor appetite / fatigue** — multifactorial (malignancy, chronic anemia).
5. **Goals of care / code status** — DNR, no aggressive interventions, home-based comfort care.

## Plan

**Administrative/Certification:**
- Two-physician hospice certification completed with attending physician.
- Formal notifications sent to primary care physician and insurer; hospice now coordinating care.
- Individualized plan of care to be developed documenting patient's goals in her own words: home, comfort, flowers, cat (Biscuit named per patient request).
- Patient's resuscitation preferences (no CPR, no machines, comfort measures only) to be documented in plan of care for all team members.

**Symptom Management:**
- **Pain:** Continue current analgesia; nurse to create written medication schedule clarifying purpose of each medication and identifying breakthrough vs. scheduled agents. Escalate scheduling/dosing as needed for comfort.
- **Bowels:** Assess bowel function every visit given opioid use and malignancy; continue fluids and prune juice; report promptly if bowel movements stop.
- **Fatigue/appetite:** Nonpharmacologic approach—pace activities to patient's best hours (mornings for gardening), permit rest; avoid unnecessary interventions.

**Care Coordination/Services:**
- Skilled nursing visits ~2x/week initially, increasing as needed. Next nursing visit in 2 days.
- Home health aide offered for bathing/dressing.
- Social worker and chaplain available per patient preference.
- 24/7 hospice on-call line provided to patient and family.

**Equipment:**
- Grab bars for bathroom — approved by patient.
- Hospital bed — declined at this time; patient prefers own bed. Revisit at patient's discretion.

**Follow-up:**
- Nurse to return in 2 days.
- Family instructed to call 24/7 line with any concerns.
