# SOAP Note

## Subjective

**Chief Complaint:** New positive pregnancy test; establishing prenatal care.

**History of Present Illness:**
Patient is a G1P0 presenting for initial prenatal intake. Last menstrual period did not occur at the end of May. After two weeks of amenorrhea, she took three home pregnancy tests (different brands), all positive. Pregnancy is planned and desired.

Reports the following first-trimester symptoms:
- Morning nausea lasting until ~10 AM; two episodes of emesis
- Breast tenderness
- Significant fatigue (fell asleep at desk earlier this week)
- Urinary frequency
- Occasional mild "pulling" twinges in lower abdomen, non-painful

**Denies:** Vaginal bleeding or spotting, painful cramping, dysuria, fever.

**Past Medical History:**
- Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis, diagnosed at age 13; no surgery required; symptomatic with prolonged standing
- Anxiety disorder with panic attacks; episodic, worse over recent months; had a mild panic attack in parking lot prior to visit. Not currently on medication; manages with breathing techniques and routines.

**Past Surgical History:** None.

**Allergies:** No known allergies.

**Social History:**
- Former smoker; quit years ago
- Occasional wine; discontinued alcohol upon positive pregnancy test
- No other substance use
- Lives with partner (Jess), who accompanies her and provides support
- Significant transportation barrier: no working vehicle; borrowed transportation; public transit is ~1.5 hours with a transfer

## Objective

**In-office urine pregnancy test:** Positive.

**Abdominal Exam:** Fundal palpation performed; uterine size consistent with gestational age.

**Pelvic Exam:** Performed; Pap smear collected. Findings appropriate for stage.

**Doppler:** Fetal heart tones present with rapid rate.

**Ultrasound (this morning):** Single intrauterine pregnancy with strong cardiac activity, measuring consistent with dates.

**Laboratory (resulted):**
- CBC: Anemia with low red blood cells

**Labs ordered/collected today:**
- Blood type and antibody screen
- CBC
- Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, HIV, syphilis (RPR), gonorrhea, chlamydia
- Rubella and varicella immunity
- Urine culture, urine protein and glucose
- Pap smear (collected during exam)

## Assessment

1. **Intrauterine pregnancy, first trimester** — single, viable, size consistent with dates; confirmed by ultrasound and positive urine testing. G1P0.
2. **Anemia of pregnancy** — low RBC on CBC; symptomatic (fatigue); caught early, treatable.
3. **Anxiety disorder with panic attacks** — not currently on medication; situational triggers include crowded waiting rooms.
4. **Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis** — history of; monitor for worsening back symptoms as pregnancy progresses.
5. **Nausea/vomiting of pregnancy** — mild, tolerable.
6. **Psychosocial: Transportation barrier** — significant obstacle to attending appointments.

## Plan

**Anemia:**
- Ferrous sulfate 325 mg PO daily; take with vitamin C source (e.g., orange juice); avoid coffee, tea, and milk around dosing. Counseled on possible constipation and dark stools.
- Vitamin B12 injection — first dose to be administered today before discharge.
- Recheck CBC over course of care.

**Anxiety:**
- May request quieter waiting area; chart flagged with front desk.
- Partner welcome at all visits, including labor.
- Reassess frequency/severity at each visit; medication options in pregnancy available if needed.

**Scoliosis:**
- Continue gentle movement, stretching, supportive footwear.
- Report worsening back pain rather than tolerating it.
- Will discuss implications for labor positioning and analgesia later in pregnancy.

**Nausea:**
- Small frequent meals; crackers before rising; cold foods to avoid triggering smells; ginger tea as desired.
- Call if unable to tolerate fluids for a full day.

**Screening/Monitoring:**
- Complete prenatal lab panel as above.
- TB skin test read at nurse visit in 2–3 days.

**Transportation accommodation:**
- Nurse visit scheduled for first morning slot for TB read.
- Appointments will be stacked/scheduled in advance to minimize trips.

**Follow-up:**
- Routine prenatal visits every 4 weeks, increasing in frequency later in pregnancy.
- Nurse visit in 2–3 days (TB read).

**Return precautions:** Call immediately for vaginal bleeding, severe cramping, fever, or inability to keep fluids down.
