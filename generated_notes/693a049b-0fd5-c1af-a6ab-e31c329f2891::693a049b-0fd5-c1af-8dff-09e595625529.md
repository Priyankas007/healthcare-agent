## Subjective

He also carries a history of ischemic heart disease with abnormal cardiac and coronary imaging. He experiences chest tightness relieved by sublingual nitroglycerin spray, which he uses approximately twice weekly, typically triggered by exertion (e.g., stairs) or emotional stress, with relief in a few minutes.

- **Appetite:** decreased.
- **Abdominal discomfort:** intermittent dull, heavy sensation in the lower abdomen; not severe.
- No acute pain today beyond the above; denies severe/uncontrolled pain.

**Goals of care (patient's words):** Time on the porch in the mornings, listening to his jazz records in the afternoon, his wife's cooking, and quiet. Wishes to remain comfortable and avoid time in waiting rooms/hospital settings.

**Code status / preferences:** Patient clearly states he wants no resuscitative or heroic measures — no CPR, no mechanical ventilation, no ambulance/emergency transport. Wife is aware and in full agreement; couple reports extensive prior discussion.

**Social:** Married 51 years; wife is primary caregiver and manages his medications. Socially isolated by preference; content with a small social circle. Wife reports the nights are her main source of worry.
