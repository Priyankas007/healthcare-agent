# Clinical Note

## Subjective

**Chief Complaint:** Progressive shortness of breath and fever in a patient with confirmed COVID-19.

**History of Present Illness:**
Mrs. Runolfsson is an 81-year-old woman presenting with a 5–6 day history of symptoms beginning shortly after the holiday. Illness started with a dry cough, followed by fever that has persisted. She subsequently developed decreased oral intake and worsening dyspnea. On the morning of presentation, she was unable to walk from bed to the bathroom without stopping twice to catch her breath. She tested positive for SARS-CoV-2 at urgent care and was advised to isolate; symptoms continued to worsen, prompting the current visit.

She describes chest tightness ("like the air is thin") but denies chest pain or pressure, and states this is distinct from her prior heart attack. Denies hemoptysis, leg swelling, falls, or fainting. Family reports she seemed "a little foggy" this morning, but the patient remains alert and interactive.

History obtained with assistance from daughter (Teresa) at bedside.

**Past Medical History:**
- Hypertension
- Prior ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI), years ago
- Hyperlipidemia
- Metabolic syndrome
- Prediabetes
- Osteoporosis
- Obesity
- Scoliosis (since adolescence)

**Allergies:** Tree nuts (walnut — prior significant swelling/reaction).

**Medications:**
- Aspirin 81 mg daily
- Atenolol 50 mg daily
- Rosuvastatin 40 mg daily
- Lisinopril 20 mg daily

Family reports strong medication adherence.

## Objective

**Vital Signs (on arrival):**
- SpO2: ~87% (subsequently dropped to ~76% while supine)
- Temperature: 38.5°C (trending upward toward 39°C)
- Heart rate: 50s on arrival, increased to high 80s
- Blood pressure: 105/53

After application of oxygen mask, SpO2 improved to low 90s.

**Diagnostics:**
- SARS-CoV-2 testing positive at urgent care and repeated positive in the ED (diagnosis confirmed).
- Chest X-ray: findings consistent with pneumonia (viral inflammation of the lungs).

**Laboratory Findings (initial):**
- WBC: mildly low
- Platelets: on the lower side
- Creatinine: elevated at 2.7 with markedly reduced estimated GFR
- Potassium: slightly elevated

**Exam/General:** Patient alert, oriented, and interactive. No acute distress noted after oxygen initiation. Improved oxygenation on monitor with mask.

## Assessment

81-year-old woman with confirmed COVID-19 complicated by viral pneumonia and hypoxemic respiratory failure. Given her age and comorbidities (hypertension, prior STEMI, metabolic syndrome/prediabetes, obesity), she is at high risk for severe disease. Clinical status is worsening with progressive hypoxemia, climbing fever, and rising heart rate.

**Active Problems:**
1. **COVID-19 with viral pneumonia** — confirmed by repeat testing and CXR; hypoxemic respiratory failure requiring supplemental oxygen.
2. **Acute kidney injury** — creatinine 2.7 with low eGFR; likely multifactorial (fever, poor oral intake, illness). Associated mild hyperkalemia.
3. **Cardiac risk** — prior STEMI; severe infection poses risk for cardiac events.
4. **Leukopenia and thrombocytopenia** — consistent with viral illness; to be monitored.
5. **Comorbidities** — HTN, hyperlipidemia, metabolic syndrome, prediabetes, osteoporosis, obesity, scoliosis (relevant to prone positioning).

## Plan

**Disposition:** Admit to isolation room. Visitors not permitted in room; daily video calls to be facilitated by nursing. Physician to call daughter (Teresa) each evening with updates.

**Respiratory:**
- Continuous supplemental oxygen via mask, titrated to maintain safe saturations.
- Prone positioning sessions daily, as tolerated, with attention to spinal/hip comfort given scoliosis (padding, chest/pelvis pillows, swimmer arm position, scheduled rotation).

**Monitoring/Labs:**
- Daily morning labs: CBC, chemistry panel.
- Scheduled additional studies: troponin (cardiac history), coagulation studies (thrombotic risk of COVID-19), iron studies.
- Consolidate draws to a single daily stick when possible; rotate venipuncture sites.
- Close monitoring of renal function and potassium.

**Renal/Fluids:**
- Gentle fluid resuscitation and monitoring.
- Cautious/deliberate management of antihypertensive medications while kidneys recover, adjusted based on daily labs.

**Cardiac:**
- Continue aspirin and cardiac medications as labs allow.
- Serial troponin monitoring.

**Medications:** Continue home regimen (ASA, atenolol, rosuvastatin, lisinopril) with adjustments guided by renal function.

**Safety/Allergy:**
- Tree nut allergy flagged to dietary services and noted on armband.
- Patient instructed to use call button immediately for worsening dyspnea, sensation that oxygen is inadequate, chest pain, or palpitations.

**Communication:** One-day-at-a-time approach discussed with patient and daughter; both verbalized understanding.
