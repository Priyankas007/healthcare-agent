# Clinical Note — Initial Prenatal Visit

## Subjective

**Chief Complaint:** New pregnancy, first prenatal visit.

**History of Present Illness:**
Ms. Melodee is a 21-year-old female, G1, presenting for her first prenatal visit accompanied by her boyfriend for support. She reports a missed period followed by two home pregnancy tests that were positive approximately two weeks ago. By her last menstrual period (mid-January) she estimates gestational age at approximately 9–10 weeks. This is her first pregnancy. She reports significant anxiety related to the pregnancy.

**Pregnancy Review of Systems:**
- **Bleeding/Spotting:** None
- **Cramping:** No significant cramping; occasional low, mild pulling "twinges"
- **Nausea/Vomiting:** Significant nausea, predominantly mornings, with several episodes of vomiting; managing with crackers and ginger ale
- **Urinary:** Increased frequency; no dysuria
- **Fevers:** None
- **Headaches/Vision changes:** Only her baseline neck-related headaches; no new headaches or visual changes

**Substance Use:**
- No smoking history
- One glass of wine at a birthday prior to knowing she was pregnant; none since
- Denies other substance use

**Relevant History — Chronic Pain:**
Chronic neck and low back pain following a fall from a horse (barrel racing) approximately two years ago. Pain severity fluctuates from mild to debilitating. Pain has limited her ability to maintain employment. Currently on:
- Hydrocodone/acetaminophen 5 mg — for severe pain days
- Tramadol 50 mg — for moderate pain days

Patient reports taking these "most days" recently, though not every day. She expressed significant fear about medication effects on the pregnancy and nearly discontinued medications abruptly two weeks ago; her boyfriend intervened. She has not stopped the medications.

**Social:** Recently completed her degree; not currently working due to chronic pain. Rides horses/barrel races. Strong partner support.

## Assessment

1. **Intrauterine pregnancy, approximately 9–10 weeks gestation, G1** — confirmed viable by ultrasound with cardiac activity; size consistent with dates.
2. **Chronic opioid use in pregnancy** (hydrocodone/acetaminophen and tramadol) for chronic neck and low back pain — requires coordinated, gradual taper; abrupt discontinuation contraindicated.
3. **Nausea and vomiting of pregnancy** — currently manageable with conservative measures.
4. **Chronic neck and low back pain** — post-traumatic (equestrian fall).
5. **Anxiety related to pregnancy** — situational; no acute risk features described.

## Plan

**Opioid Management / Chronic Pain:**
- Continue current medications at the lowest functional dose; **do NOT stop abruptly** (withdrawal risk to patient and fetus discussed).
- Provider to contact pain specialist this week to collaboratively develop a gradual, written taper schedule.
- Introduce pregnancy-safe non-pharmacologic pain strategies: physical therapy, heat, stretching, activity pacing, and positioning adjustments.
- Counseled on risk of neonatal withdrawal with continued opioid use and rationale for taper.

**Nausea/Vomiting:**
- Small, frequent meals; fluids between rather than with meals.
- Contact office if unable to keep food/fluids down (will treat; advised not to "tough it out").

**Activity/Safety:**
- **Discontinue horseback riding** (fall risk) for the duration of pregnancy. Ground activities with horses permitted.
- Encourage walking, gentle stretching, and adequate sleep.

**Anxiety/Stress:**
- Reassured everyday stress is not harmful to the pregnancy.
- Will screen and check in at each visit; referral to support resources available if needed.

- Chlamydia and gonorrhea swabs
- Rubella and varicella immunity
- Pap smear (with exam)
