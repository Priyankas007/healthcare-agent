# OB Initial Prenatal Visit Note

## Subjective

**Chief Complaint:** New pregnancy, initial prenatal visit.

**History of Present Illness:**
Patient presents for her first prenatal visit accompanied by her husband. She reports a missed period (typically regular cycles) prompting home pregnancy testing, with three positive home tests. Since then she reports:
- Fatigue that "sneaks up" on her
- Mild morning nausea when she waits too long to eat, relieved by eating crackers before rising
- No vomiting she cannot keep ahead of
- No dizziness or fainting (reports only wooziness when hungry)
- No vaginal bleeding or spotting
- No cramping or abdominal pain; occasional mild low "pulling/stretching" sensation, not painful
- Increased urinary frequency; no dysuria/burning
- No fevers

**Relevant History:**
- **Recurrent UTIs:** History of at least a couple of UTIs per year since her twenties. Expresses significant concern about developing a UTI during pregnancy.
- Recently stopped birth control when planning conception.

**Medications:** No regular medications. Occasional over-the-counter analgesic for headache.

**Social History:**
- No alcohol since positive pregnancy test
- Never smoked
- No recreational drug use
- Consumes approximately one cup of coffee daily

## Objective

**Office Urine Pregnancy Test:** Positive

**Physical Exam:** General exam healthy and normal per provider. Uterus size consistent with early gestational age. Fundal height measured (baseline).

**Ultrasound:** Intrauterine pregnancy confirmed, located centrally within the uterus. Cardiac activity present ("flicker"), described as strong and steady. Viable pregnancy.

**Fetal Heart Tones:** Auscultated, reassuring.

## Assessment

- Confirmed intrauterine pregnancy, early gestation, viable — normal and healthy at this time.
- History of recurrent urinary tract infections — increased surveillance warranted during pregnancy.

## Plan

**Diagnostic Workup (today):**
- Urine culture sent (to be repeated routinely at prenatal visits given recurrent UTI history; asymptomatic bacteriuria to be treated promptly with pregnancy-safe antibiotics)
- Blood work: blood type, CBC (anemia screen), infection panel (hepatitis B, hepatitis C, HIV, syphilis), rubella and varicella immunity
- Urine testing for chlamydia and gonorrhea
- Urine screen for glucose and protein
- Pap smear (patient due)
- Tuberculosis skin test placed on forearm; requires reading in 2–3 days

*Patient advised she is a historical fainter; blood draw to be performed lying down with juice available.*

**Patient Education / Counseling:**
- Review medication list; instructed to check with provider before taking any new medication, including OTC
- One cup of coffee per day acceptable; avoid alcohol, smoking, and recreational substances
- Continue normal activities, diet, work, and exercise (walks)
- Cracker strategy for nausea; rest as needed
- **Call same day** for: burning with urination, urgency beyond usual, unilateral back pain, or fever
- Timing of announcing pregnancy is patient's personal decision; no medical restriction

**Follow-up:**
- Prenatal visits every 4 weeks for now, increasing in frequency later in pregnancy
- Nurse to draw labs, place TB test, and schedule: (1) nurse visit this week for TB test reading, (2) next prenatal visit
- Lab results to be posted to patient portal; office will call regarding any actionable results
