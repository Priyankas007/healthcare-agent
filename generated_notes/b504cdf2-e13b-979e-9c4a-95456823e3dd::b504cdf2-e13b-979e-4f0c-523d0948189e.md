# Clinical Note — Admission (SNF/Rehabilitation)

## Subjective

Mrs. Hilpert is a 76-year-old woman admitted to Maple Grove for rehabilitation following approximately one week of hospitalization. She reports significant deconditioning, describing profound fatigue and functional decline in her legs to the point of being unable to ambulate to her mailbox at the time of hospital discharge.

**Chief complaint:** Generalized weakness and low back/hip pain limiting mobility.

**History of present illness:**
- **Pain:** Low back pain radiating to bilateral hips, worse with prolonged standing. Described as a deep, aching, "toothache in the bone" sensation. Standing is the primary aggravating factor. Pain has interfered with sleep on some nights. Requires support and a target to hold onto when standing. Can turn in bed slowly. Reports a cycle of pain leading to prolonged sitting, which contributes to further weakness.
- She reports fatigue related to the hospital stay and the transport.

**Past medical history (per patient and records):**
- Osteoporosis
- Hyperlipidemia
- Prediabetes
- Significant dental loss — chews carefully on one side, limited to soft foods.

**Nutrition:** Reports reduced intake over years due to poor dentition and cooking for one; diet often limited to toast and tea. Willing to eat soft-textured foods.

**Social history:**
- Lives alone. No family support nearby; sister died two winters ago. A neighbor assists with taking out bins.
- History of a prior marriage that she left; states leaving was the right decision and cost her most of her social connections.
- **Safety screen:** Denies any current fear of the former spouse or any other person; states he has been out of her life for years and there is no current safety concern.
- Reports social isolation ("the quiet").

## Objective

- Patient arrived by transport approximately one hour prior to evaluation; transport paperwork received.
- Alert, oriented, and engaged; conversant and coherent throughout interview.
- Full history and physical examination to be completed at time of admission (as stated during visit).

## Assessment

76-year-old woman admitted for post-hospitalization rehabilitation with:

1. **Deconditioning / impaired functional mobility** — significant lower extremity weakness limiting ambulation and safe standing.
2. **Chronic low back and bilateral hip pain** — mechanical/positional, worsened by standing, interfering with therapy participation and sleep.
3. **Osteoporosis** — elevated fall risk; fractures would be high-consequence events.
4. **Hyperlipidemia.**
5. **Prediabetes.**
6. **Poor dentition / likely inadequate nutritional intake** — at risk for malnutrition, relevant to strength recovery and bone health.
7. **Social isolation / limited home support** — relevant to safe discharge planning.

## Plan

**Pain management:**
- Start **naproxen 220 mg** for baseline aching pain.
- Start **meperidine 50 mg** tablets for breakthrough/severe pain (e.g., pre-therapy, severe nighttime pain), used sparingly at the lowest effective dose.
- Counseled patient on risks in older adults: drowsiness, cognitive fogginess, unsteadiness, and fall risk. Nursing to monitor closely after each dose.
- Patient instructed to report any undesirable cognitive/sedating effects; regimen will be adjusted as needed.

**Fall prevention / safety:**
- Call button before every stand, at all times including overnight.
- PT to instruct on safe transfer and mobility techniques.
- Nursing fall-risk precautions given osteoporosis.

**Therapy / rehabilitation:**
- **Physical therapy:** standing, gait training, lower extremity strengthening.
- **Occupational therapy:** dressing, safe bathing, kitchen/ADL tasks.

**Nutrition:**
- **Dietitian consult:** soft/manageable textures with adequate nourishment; emphasis on protein for strength and calcium for bone health; balanced for prediabetes and hyperlipidemia.
- Nursing to document texture preferences and communicate to kitchen tonight.

**Ancillary / social work:**
- **Social work consult** for: (1) discharge support planning, and (2) addressing social isolation — community programs/senior center resources.
- Safety history documented once in chart per patient's disclosure to prevent repeated questioning.

**Nursing:**
- Complete admission assessment.
- Every-shift monitoring of pain, blood pressure, skin, and medications.

**Care planning / disposition:**
- Team to develop individualized plan of care.
- Anticipated length of stay approximately two weeks, dependent on strength recovery.
- **Pre-discharge assessment** required prior to discharge: ability to stand/walk, manage medications and meals, and confirmation of adequate home support.

**Patient understanding:** Patient engaged, verbalized understanding of the plan, and agreed to use the call button and to report medication side effects.
