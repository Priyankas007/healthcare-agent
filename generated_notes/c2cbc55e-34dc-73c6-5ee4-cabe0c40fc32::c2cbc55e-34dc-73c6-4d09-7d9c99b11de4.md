# Prenatal Visit — SOAP Note

## Subjective

**Chief Complaint:** Positive home pregnancy tests; establishing prenatal care.

**HPI:**
Ms. Margarita is a 43-year-old female presenting for an initial prenatal visit. Reports irregular menstrual cycles at baseline. At approximately 10 weeks since last menstrual period, she took three home pregnancy tests over a two-day period, all strongly positive. Estimated gestational age approximately 10 weeks by LMP.

Patient reports mixed emotions regarding the pregnancy — it was unplanned, and she has anxiety related to a prior early pregnancy loss. She feels supported and is engaged in care.

**Pregnancy-related symptoms:**
- Breast tenderness
- Afternoon fatigue
- Aversion to certain smells (notably coffee)
- Morning queasiness, no vomiting; tolerating food (crackers)
- Increased urinary frequency
- **Denies:** vaginal bleeding, spotting, cramping, headaches, vision changes, swelling of hands/face, dysuria

**Past Obstetric History:**
- Prior first-trimester miscarriage (years ago; no clinical care sought at the time)

**Past Medical History:**
- Hypertension (since her 30s)
- Prediabetes
- Anemia
- Obesity
- Metabolic syndrome
- Family history: mother developed diabetes in her 50s

**Medications:** None currently (including no OTC, herbals). Reports lapse in hypertension management.

**Social/Safety:**
- Screened for intimate partner violence privately. Reports history of abuse by ex-husband (now living three states away). Currently remarried; reports feeling safe at home with supportive spouse.
- IPV resources provided per patient acceptance.

## Objective

**Physical Exam:** Physical exam, fundal height measurement, and pelvic exam with Pap smear planned/performed as part of visit.

**Doppler:** Fetal heart tones auscultated.

**Transvaginal/Pelvic Ultrasound (viability scan):**
- Intrauterine pregnancy, appropriately located
- Cardiac activity present ("strong and steady")
- Findings consistent with a normal, healthy early pregnancy

**In-office testing:** Urine pregnancy test confirmed positive.

*(No vital signs documented in transcript.)*

## Assessment

1. **Intrauterine pregnancy, approximately 10 weeks**, confirmed viable with documented fetal cardiac activity on ultrasound and Doppler — normal early pregnancy.
2. **Advanced maternal age (43)** — pregnancy at increased risk.
3. **Chronic hypertension** — currently untreated; increased risk for preeclampsia.
4. **Prediabetes with family history of diabetes** — increased risk for gestational diabetes.
5. **Anemia.**
6. **Obesity / metabolic syndrome.**
7. **History of prior first-trimester pregnancy loss** — patient anxiety, reassured today.
8. **History of intimate partner violence (prior relationship)** — currently safe.

## Plan

**Diagnostics / Labs ordered:**
- Blood type
- Complete blood count (also to assess anemia)
- Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, HIV, syphilis screening
- Rubella and varicella immunity
- Chlamydia and gonorrhea swabs
- Urine culture, urine protein, urine glucose screen
- Pap smear
- Tuberculosis skin test (forearm) — recheck in 2–3 days
- Results to be released via patient portal

**Monitoring / Follow-up:**
- Prenatal visits monthly initially, increasing in frequency later; closer surveillance given advanced maternal age and hypertension.
- Continued screening for gestational diabetes throughout pregnancy given prediabetes/family history.
- Next visit in ~4 weeks.

**Patient education / Counseling:**
- Precautionary symptoms to report immediately (day or night): headaches, visual disturbances, sudden swelling of hands/face, vaginal bleeding, cramping, intractable vomiting, or any persistent concern.
- Lifestyle: regular meals, vegetables, iron-rich foods (beans, greens, red meat) for anemia, gentle activity (walking), adequate sleep.
- Optional genetic screening to be discussed in detail at next visit; informational materials to be provided.
- Partner welcome at future visits, including ultrasounds.
- IPV resource card provided; office identified as a safe resource if circumstances change.
