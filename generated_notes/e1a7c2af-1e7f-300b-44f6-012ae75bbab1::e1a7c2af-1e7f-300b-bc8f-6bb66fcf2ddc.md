# SOAP Note

## Subjective

**Chief Complaint:** Gum bleeding, halitosis, and cold sensitivity; dental readiness referral.

**HPI:** 35-year-old active duty male, generally healthy, presents via clinic referral for evaluation after many years without dental care (attributed to base moves and a deployment). Reports gingival bleeding with brushing for several months, occurring most times he brushes and worse along the lower anterior region; persists even with gentle brushing. Also reports recent halitosis (noted by a peer) and cold sensitivity, particularly to ice water on the right side.

Denies toothache, night pain, pain with chewing, loose teeth, facial or jaw swelling, and fevers.

**Habits/Social:** Brushes twice daily but quickly; flosses inconsistently. Never used tobacco (never smoked or dipped). Frequent energy drink consumption, often sipped over extended periods.

**Family History:** Grandfather with dentures at approximately age 50.

## Objective

**Extraoral:** No facial or jaw swelling reported/observed.

**Intraoral Exam:**
- Gingiva diffusely erythematous and edematous
- Bleeding on light touch (marginal, generalized)
- Generalized supragingival calculus along the gumline
- Subgingival calculus palpable, especially lower anterior and posterior molar regions
- No mobile teeth noted
- Localized area of overgrown/pocketed gingival tissue on the lower right

**Imaging:** Bitewing radiographs obtained. Bone levels sound; no alarming interproximal findings.

## Assessment

- **Gingival disease (gingivitis)** — generalized, plaque/calculus induced, without radiographic evidence of bone loss. Caught at a reversible stage.
- **Localized gingival overgrowth/pseudopocketing, lower right** — requiring surgical management.
- **Cold (thermal) sensitivity**, right side.

## Plan

**Procedures completed today:**
- Full-mouth scaling above and below the gumline (deep cleaning) under local anesthesia
- Localized gingivectomy of lower right overgrown/pocketed tissue under same anesthesia
- Application of desensitizing medicament to sensitive areas
- Fluoride treatment

**Post-op / Home Care Instructions:**
- No eating for ~30 minutes after fluoride
- Soft diet today; avoid scalding hot foods and sharp/crusty foods; caution while numb
- Avoid chewing on lower right (surgical) side today
- Warm salt-water rinses beginning tomorrow for lower right
- Continue brushing despite minor bleeding; soft-bristle brush, twice daily, two minutes, bristles angled into gumline with small circular motions
- Floss once daily
- Expected: soreness at lower right for a couple of days; some bleeding during healing for first week
- Morning PT/exercise permitted; take it easy tonight

**Habit Counseling:**
- Reduce energy drink use; if consumed, drink promptly rather than sipping over hours; water in between

**Return Precautions:** Call office same day for significant swelling, pus, fever, or bleeding not controlled with pressure.

**Follow-up:** Return in a few weeks to assess healing and gingival response to new oral hygiene regimen. Follow-up to be scheduled at front desk.
